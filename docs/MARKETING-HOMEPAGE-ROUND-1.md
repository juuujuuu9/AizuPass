# Marketing Homepage — Round 1 Concepts

> Compiled: 2026-03-28

## Summary

Three early-stage homepage concepts for AizuPass, each targeting a distinct ICP segment identified in [PRODUCT-STRATEGY.md](./PRODUCT-STRATEGY.md). All three are grounded in the principles from [MARKETING-HOMEPAGE-PRINCIPLES.md](./MARKETING-HOMEPAGE-PRINCIPLES.md), the content map in [MARKETING-HOMEPAGE-CONTENT-MAP.md](./MARKETING-HOMEPAGE-CONTENT-MAP.md), and the Tapcart case study in [MARKETING-IDEAS.md](./MARKETING-IDEAS.md). Copy is honest-bar compliant — no unearned metrics or claims flagged as Missing/Partial in [MASTER-PLAN.md](./MASTER-PLAN.md).

**Recommendation:** Start with **Variant 3 (The Switcher)** as the default homepage — concrete, comparison-SEO-friendly, and testable. Graduate to Variant 1 (The Release Drop) once a culture-adjacent pilot case study exists.

---

## Variant 1 — "The Release Drop"

**ICP:** Independent music collectives, promoters, small shows (50–250 guests, Segment A in PRODUCT-STRATEGY.md)

**Emotional hook:** Identity — "built for how we actually do events, not a box office"

**Headline:** Your door should feel like your event.

**Subhead:** QR check-in, RSVPs, and guest lists built for shows — not box offices. Fast setup. Offline-safe. Your crowd, not Eventbrite's.

**Tagline:** *Fast door. Your list. No platform in between.*

**CTA pair**
- Primary: Start free
- Secondary: See how check-in works

---

### Sections

#### 1. You're not a venue. Don't use venue software.

Big platforms put their logo on your invites, email your guests about competing shows, and take 7 days to pay you. AizuPass doesn't know your crowd. We just move them through the door.

#### 2. Your guests are yours.

No discovery feed. No "related events." Your attendee list lives in your account, not ours. Export it anytime.

#### 3. The door, done.

QR scan → green. Already checked in → amber. Not on the list → red. Works in dead-zone venues without Wi-Fi. One tap if the QR won't scan.

#### 4. Set up faster than a flyer.

Import a CSV, share a link, scan QR. 2 minutes start to finish — not 20.

#### 5. Paid like you mean it.

Same fees as Eventbrite (3.5% + $1.79). Get paid 48 hours after ticket sale on Pro, daily on Business. Not 7 days after the event ends.

---

### Tone & language notes

- Match invite/flyer energy — short sentences, confident
- Use: doors, room, crowd, release, tiers, early access
- Avoid: attendees, workflow, solution, platform (except as contrast)

## Variant 2 — "The Activation"

**ICP:** Agencies running private/brand activations (150–500 attendees, Segment B in PRODUCT-STRATEGY.md)

**Emotional hook:** Professional credibility — "ops your client can see in the recap deck"

**Headline:** Invite-only check-in your client can put their name on.

**Subhead:** Private RSVPs, tiered guest lists, and door ops for brand activations. Their branding. Your control. No ticketing platform in the room.

**Tagline:** *RSVP, tiers, and check-in — your client on every surface.*

**CTA pair**
- Primary: Request access
- Secondary: See a sample activation

---

### Sections

#### 1. Client-branded from invite to door.

Logo, colors, custom domain. The experience reads as their event, not a third-party tool. No "Powered by Eventbrite" anywhere your client's eye lands.

#### 2. Control who gets in.

Tiered lists (VIP / GA / press / staff). Approval/deny flows. Capacity caps. Plus-ones that follow rules. No public listing, ever.

#### 3. Post-event proof in the recap.

Check-in timestamps, headcount, completion rate. Built-in ops metrics that belong in the deck.

#### 4. Multiple staff, one view.

Assign door scanners, each in their lane. Real-time sync when online; offline queue when the venue's network is what it always is.

#### 5. Their data. Your account. No platform in between.

Guest data doesn't feed someone else's marketing. Export clean CSVs for your CRM. Move it anywhere.

---

### Tone & language notes

- Professional, ops-confident, agency-fluent
- Use: activation, RSVP, tier, approval, recap deck, capacity
- Avoid: tickets (if free/invite), sign-up, conference language

## Variant 3 — "The Switcher"

**ICP:** Organizers currently on Eventbrite who are frustrated by cash flow, data ownership, or competitor discovery

**Emotional hook:** Cash flow + resentment — speaks to a felt pain, not a hypothetical one

**Headline:** Same fees as Eventbrite. Get paid 5 days faster.

**Subhead:** AizuPass matches Eventbrite's ticket fees exactly — and pays out in 48 hours, not after the event ends. Plus your guest data is yours, and they stop emailing your crowd.

**Tagline:** *Switch for the cash flow. Stay for the control.*

**CTA pair**
- Primary: Start your first event
- Secondary: Compare to Eventbrite

---

### Sections

#### 1. Side by side

| | Eventbrite | AizuPass |
|---|---|---|
| Ticket fees | 3.5% + $1.79 + 2.9% processing | Same |
| Payout timing | After event ends | 48 hrs (Pro) / daily (Business) |
| Guest data | Eventbrite's to market | Yours — export anytime |
| Discovery feed | Competing events shown to your guests | No listing, ever |
| Branding | Their logo, their emails | Your logo, your domain |

#### 2. Your list is a business asset.

Eventbrite markets to your guests. We don't. Your attendee emails live in your account. Export them, take them to your next platform, use them in your CRM.

#### 3. Check-in that actually works at the door.

QR scan, instant green/amber/red. Works offline in venues where Wi-Fi is theoretical. Manual name lookup when QR fails.

#### 4. Starts free. Pricing you can explain.

Free tier for your first event (up to 150 guests). Pro at $39/month unlocks faster payouts, custom branding, and unlimited staff. No "contact us."

---

### Tone & language notes

- Slightly confrontational, numbers-forward, respect the buyer's intelligence
- Lead with money first — cash flow is what makes switchers actually switch
- Use: guest list, payout, export, your data
- Avoid: enterprise-speak, buzzwords, vague "control" without a concrete example

## Shared structural rules (all variants)

Drawn from [MARKETING-HOMEPAGE-PRINCIPLES.md](./MARKETING-HOMEPAGE-PRINCIPLES.md) and [MARKETING-IDEAS.md](./MARKETING-IDEAS.md):

- One primary CTA above the fold; repeat once after first full scroll
- Never three equal buttons — hierarchy: primary → secondary
- Dual CTA pattern: low-friction self-serve + high-touch demo/walkthrough
- Show the product (scanner UI, traffic-light states) as early as credibility allows
- Attach proof to specific features, not only to a footer testimonials block
- No fake metrics — only claim what is measured and true (see honesty bar in MASTER-PLAN.md)
- Replace generic testimonials with a single pilot quote until real customers exist
